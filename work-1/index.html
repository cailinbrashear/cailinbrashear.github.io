I have had my fair share of struggles while conducting these projects to say the least. Upon entering this class, I had zero experience with HTML or CSS, and, as someone who finds herself struggling to create simple WordPress pages, I was nervous. Now, with many hours of constantly refreshing and scanning my screen to see why on earth a change happened that I did not anticipate to, I can say that I am beginning to learn a lot about HTML and CSS as a whole. The satisfaction of typing in small words, phrases, and numbers and having it change before your eyes into real visual effects is comparable to fixing the headlight on your car – real work that elicits a visible effect. I do have to say, the feelings of accomplishment that follow doing something right is well worth the struggle. This is a skill that I never expected to have, and, even with the small amount of knowledge I have learned thus far, I feel as if I am developing hard skills that will benefit me in the future. And that’s the reason I took this class in the first place.

Most of my original works needed a good deal of up-keep to add to my website. First, I updated my about me – adding subjects and a voice that I would want to exemplify me better to my audience as well as thoroughly expanding what I wanted my website to portray. I still feel as if this section will need some work, but I feel as if I need to start adding real content before finalizing this section. I completely revised my embedded fiction assignment. I first began with a yelp review, and then changed it to an AirBnb review with basically the same premise, but that I feel relates better to the topic of imbedded fiction and is more relevant in my own life. Finally, I revised my envisioning my design draft by creating more clear, concrete ideas and scratching abstract, vague topics in order to get a better feel for what I really wanted my website to be. I changed my theme slightly, narrowed it a bit and made it less rough around the edges so that it would result in a more cohesive and coherent vision to my audience. I used many strategies, most through trial and error, to integrate and practice web design principles through crafting my website. First off, I began slowly with picking and changing several fonts to decide which ones I liked back, what size, and in what location on the page. I liked how the CSS only involves one, single index so that I did not have to be switching back and forth between pages to make specific changes. Crafting my main menu is what I believe to be my biggest achievement in regards to using CSS – linking every menu item into one group and changing the formatting from there. I really liked how it turned out. I also made my website very centralized with hopes that my audience’s eye will easily follow down the page with the flow of information. I did not originally intend to do this, but, after trying many different alignment options, I felt that it looked best this way. Choosing the background color was also something that I did not plan on, as I originally wanted to incorporate a good deal of white space, but adding a beige color made the site itself feel more put together, warmer, and less harsh and contrasted. For me, one of my biggest struggles was aligning my menu’s and texts the way I wanted them to be and finding just the right balance of indentations and margins that looked right on screen. I realized that, most of the time, how I wanted it to look originally did not end up being the best suited design strategy for the page; I had to allow for changes as such. Though I do believe I have come along way, I know that I have many things left to learn. I would like to learn how to possibly add animations and moving items on my webpage when the audience scrolls down or pans over certain objects. I attempted to read about the subject, but it was way over my frame of current knowledge. I also hope to learn more about photo and image photoshop so that I may fill my website with many various images that seem coherent to the theme of the webpage itself. Finally, I would like to learn graphic design skills that would allow me to envision a true logo for my website – as I believe this would be a good hard-skill to have in my life and would benefit my professional career later on.
